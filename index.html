San Ángel · Coyoacán · UNAM

Saturday, March 14
Bazar Sábado · Anahuacalli / Casa Azul · Coyoacán · Jardines UNAM
📍 Southern corridor day: San Ángel → Coyoacán → UNAM — all flow south with no backtracking. One Frida Kahlo ticket covers Anahuacalli too (10 min apart). Book Frida Kahlo timed entry weeks in advance — no walk-in sales.

Coffee and pastry at San Ángel Inn in its hacienda courtyard, or grab something quick near Plaza del Carmen before the Bazar opens
Other option maybe the crepes we went to last time?

10:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Bazar Sábado
Mexico's most famous Saturday artisan market — held in a stunning 17th-century colonial mansion. Textiles, hand-painted ceramics, silver jewellery, folk art from every region. Saturday only, indoors and outdoors. Browse Plaza del Carmen stalls afterward and peek inside Museo del Carmen — a former convent with extraordinary cloister gardens and mummified monks in the crypt.
MarketSaturday only
12:30 – 1:30 PM
Lunch at Mercado Coyoacán
Uber to Coyoacán (~15 min). The covered market is alive on Saturdays — tostadas, quesadillas, enfrijoladas. Find Carmelita's stall for the classic tostadas. Browse the chocolate tasting section and the homemade banana chip stands.
Lunch
1:30 – 2:30 PM
Coyoacán — Plaza Hidalgo & cobblestone streets
Wander the colonial neighbourhood on foot. Plaza Hidalgo and Jardín Centenario are full of street musicians, artisan stalls, and local families on Saturdays. Stop at Café Avellaneda for a coffee before the museums.
Neighbourhood
2:45 – 4:15 PM
Anahuacalli Museum or Casa Azul
Anahuacalli — Diego Rivera's extraordinary volcanic-stone pyramid, built to house his 50,000-piece pre-Hispanic art collection. The building itself is a masterpiece: Mesoamerican architecture fused with lava rock, rooftop views over Pedregal. Opens 11am, closes 5:30pm. Or begin with Casa Azul (Frida Kahlo) — timed entry, book ahead at museofridakahlo.org.mx. One ticket covers both.

4:30 – 5:30 PM
The second museum
Whichever you didn't do first — Casa Azul (Frida's blue home, her clothing, paintings, personal objects, kitchen and garden) or Anahuacalli. Only 10 min apart by Uber. Your Frida Kahlo ticket is valid for both — no extra charge. TBD
Museum1 ticket covers both
6:00 – 7:30 PM
UNAM — Jardines & Biblioteca Central
Uber south to Ciudad Universitaria. The Jardín Botánico UNAM covers 16 hectares of native Mexican plants, cacti, and volcanic rock gardens — free and open late on weekends. Then walk to the Biblioteca Central: its four facades are covered in Juan O'Gorman's monumental mosaic murals depicting all of Mexican history. One of the most photographed buildings in the world.

Centro Histórico

Monday, March 16
Downtown Tour · Bellas Artes · Zócalo · Biblioteca Vasconcelos
⚠️ Monday: Most major museums are closed (Chapultepec, Anthropology, Frida Kahlo). Perfect day for Centro Histórico — the Zócalo, Palacio Nacional, Templo Mayor, Bellas Artes, and Biblioteca Vasconcelos are all open.
8:30 – 9:30 AM
Breakfast — Café de Tacuba or Panadería Rosetta
Café de Tacuba (1912, stunning tiled dining room, right in Centro) for a full Mexican breakfast. Or grab a guava roll and cinnamon bun from Panadería Rosetta on the way.
Breakfast
9:30 – 11:00 AM
Palacio de Bellas Artes
One of the most beautiful buildings in Latin America — white Carrara marble with an art nouveau exterior and art deco interior. Inside: Diego Rivera's most important murals (including Man, Controller of the Universe), plus rotating contemporary exhibitions. The Torre Latinoamericana is right next door — take the lift to the 44th floor for a 360° panorama of the city.
Architecture · Murals
11:00 AM – 11:45 AM
Alameda Central & Palacio Postal
Stroll through Alameda Central — CDMX's oldest public park (1592), full of jacaranda trees in March. Walk five minutes to the Palacio Postal: the most jaw-dropping post office in the world — gilded Venetian staircases, ornate brass fittings, free to enter and photograph.
Walk
12:00 – 1:30 PM
Zócalo & Palacio Nacional
The enormous Plaza de la Constitución — political heart of Mexico since Aztec times, ringed by the Catedral Metropolitana (largest in the Americas) and government palaces. Walk straight into the Palacio Nacional (free) for Diego Rivera's breathtaking mural cycle wrapping the entire main stairwell — the full history of Mexico in paint.
Historic Centre
1:30 – 2:30 PM
Templo Mayor & lunch
The excavated ruins of the great Aztec temple, discovered in 1978 when electrical workers hit a stone disc. Ancient pyramid foundations sitting metres from the Colonial Cathedral — one of the great urban archaeological sites. The adjacent Museo del Templo Mayor has superb artefacts. Grab a torta or tacos from stalls along the streets of Centro for lunch.
RuinsLunch
3:30 – 5:00 PM
Biblioteca Vasconcelos
Mexico's extraordinary "megalibrary" — books float on enormous suspended shelves in a vast glass-and-steel atrium that echoes like a cathedral. There's a giant whale skeleton sculpture hanging inside. Located in Buenavista, a short metro ride or Uber from Centro. Free entry. One of the most remarkable spaces in CDMX.
Architecture
7:30 PM onwards
Dinner in Roma Norte
Contramar (tuna tostadas, grilled whole fish — arrive 30 min before opening), Masala y Maiz (Mexican-Indian fusion), or Rosetta. Finish with cocktails at Licorería Limantour (4th best bar in the world — reserve ahead).
DinnerNightlife

Chapultepec & Antropología

Tuesday, March 17
Castillo de Chapultepec · Bosque · Museo de Antropología · Reforma
⚡ Book Chapultepec Castle online to skip the 2-hr walk-up queue. The castle sits at the top of a 20-min uphill walk from the park entrance — arrive by 8:45am. No water or food allowed inside the castle.
8:00 – 8:30 AM
Breakfast

Breakfast
9:00 – 11:30 AM
Castillo de Chapultepec
The only royal castle in North America — imperial residence of Maximilian I and Carlota, then Mexico's presidents, now the National Museum of History. Extraordinary stained-glass galleries, Diego Rivera murals, imperial apartments preserved in full. Panoramic views over the entire city from the terraces. Budget 2–2.5 hrs. March often brings early jacaranda blooms on the hillside.
Castle · MuseumNo water/food inside · book online
11:30 AM – 12:15 PM
Walk Bosque de Chapultepec
Descend through one of the world's largest urban parks — lakes, vendors, families, rowboats. The Museo de Antropología is about 15 min on foot through the park from the castle. Grab a snack from a street stall on the way.
Park
12:15 – 1:15 PM
Lunch near the park
Lardo (Condesa, 10 min Uber — excellent brunch plates) or tacos from a stand at the park edge. You need energy for 3 hours in the world's greatest pre-Columbian museum.
Lunch
1:30 – 5:00 PM
Museo Nacional de Antropología
One of the greatest museums on Earth — 22 rooms of pre-Columbian civilisation across a vast courtyard complex. The Aztec Sun Stone (Piedra del Sol), towering Olmec heads, Mayan jade burial masks, Xochipilli statues, Monte Albán tomb replicas. Give yourself a minimum 3 hours. English translations throughout. The giant umbrella pillar at the centre of the courtyard is iconic.
Museum
5:30 – 6:30 PM
Walk Paseo de la Reforma → Ángel de la Independencia
Stroll the grand Hausmann-style boulevard at golden hour, past the towering golden Ángel de la Independencia. One of the great evening city walks anywhere in the world.
Walk

Essential Notes

Things to book before you arrive

  • Frida Kahlo Museum — timed-entry, no walk-in sales, sells out weeks ahead. Buy at museofridakahlo.org.mx. Your ticket includes free entry to Anahuacalli Museum.
  • Jardines de México — buy tickets at jardinesdemexico.com. Open Tue–Sun 9am–6pm, closed Mondays. Bring water; eat in Tequesquitengo nearby instead of on-site.
  • Pujol — book weeks ahead at pujol.com.mx. DEGUSTACIÓN tasting menu ~$130. OMAKASE taco bar is more casual.
  • Chapultepec Castle — buy online to skip the 2-hr queue. Open Tue–Sun 9am–5pm.
  • Licorería Limantour — reservation recommended for evenings.
  • Contramar — arrive 30 min before opening if no reservation.
  • Download the Metro CDMX app before arrival. Use Uber/Didi only — avoid unmarked taxis.
  • Pack bags Tuesday night — Wednesday flight is at noon, leave the hotel by 9am.
